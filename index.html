Heat Stacking Mechanism

El Nino remains a natural climate phenomenon, but in a warmer climate it can occur on top of already accumulated ocean heat, land heat, urban heat, WBGT stress, soil dryness, and weakened natural cooling functions.

The risk is not only an anomaly in one year. It is the stacking of a heat pulse on a higher baseline.

Carbon Accounting vs Thermal Accounting

Carbon Credits

Ask how much CO2 was reduced, removed, or offset. This remains important for emissions reduction and accounting.

Cooling Credits

Ask how much heat load was reduced, avoided, dissipated, or buffered through measurable cooling functions.

Cooling Credit Transition

Cooling Credits are proposed as a complementary and potentially transitional framework from carbon accounting alone toward thermal accounting and measurable cooling contribution.
